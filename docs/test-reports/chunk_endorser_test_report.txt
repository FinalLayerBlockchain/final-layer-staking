BACKGROUND
----------
Final Layer inherits NEAR Protocol's Nightshade sharding architecture, which
distinguishes between three validator roles:

  1. BLOCK PRODUCER
     Produces full blocks (block headers that tie all shards together).
     Only the top-N validators by stake qualify (N = num_block_producer_seats).
     Block producers also produce chunks for their assigned shards.

  2. CHUNK-ONLY PRODUCER
     Produces shard chunks but NOT full blocks.
     Validators with stake below the block producer threshold fall into this role.
     They are still validators — they produce chunks, earn rewards, and can be
     kicked out — but they never author block headers.

  3. ENDORSER (Chunk Validator / Stateless Validator)
     Attests to the validity of chunks produced by chunk producers.
     In NEAR's stateless validation model (Nightshade Phase 2+), endorsers
     verify chunk witnesses and submit endorsements.
     Validators outside the chunk producer set (if num_chunk_validator_seats
     is smaller than total validators) act as pure endorsers.

This test verifies that when num_block_producer_seats = 1 out of 3 total
validators, the protocol correctly assigns chunk-only and endorser roles.

KEY OBSERVATIONS
----------------
1. Role assignment works correctly.
   With num_block_producer_seats=1, only node0 produced block headers (blocks).
   node1 and node2 produced ZERO blocks but actively produced chunks and endorsed.

2. Chunk production is distributed across all validators.
   The total expected chunks per epoch were split between node0, node1, and node2.
   This demonstrates that chunk-only producers genuinely contribute to throughput
   even though they do not produce block headers.

3. Endorsements are universal.
   All three validators submitted endorsements for every expected slot.
   endorsements=18/18 for all three at the end of the test window.
   Endorsements are the lightweight attestation mechanism — they confirm that
   chunk witnesses are valid without requiring full state re-execution.

4. ENDORSER_ONLY role appeared intermittently for node2.
   At some epoch boundaries (epochs 1 and 3 in the monitoring window), node2
   showed num_expected_chunks=0, classifying it as ENDORSER_ONLY for that snapshot.
   This reflects epoch-to-epoch variation in chunk assignment, not a permanent role.
   By the end of the test, node2 was back to producing chunks.

5. No kickouts, no slashes throughout 7 epochs.
   All validators met their block/chunk/endorsement obligations.
   The network ran at ~4 blocks/second with 3 nodes connected.

HOW REWARDS DIFFER BY ROLE
----------------------------
  Block producer reward:
    Gets block production reward for each block authored.
    Also gets chunk production reward for each chunk in their assigned shards.

  Chunk-only producer reward:
    Gets chunk production reward only (no block production reward).
    Total reward is typically lower than a block producer of equal stake.

  Endorser reward:
    Gets endorsement reward for attesting to chunks.
    Lowest total reward per unit stake, but still contributes to network security.

  All rewards are proportional to participation rate (produced/expected ratio).
  A validator that misses >10% of expected chunks/endorsements faces kickout risk.


CONCLUSION
----------
The chunk-only producer and endorser roles function correctly in Final Layer v1003.
The protocol properly assigns roles based on stake rank and num_block_producer_seats,
distributes chunk production across all eligible validators, and collects
endorsements from all validators. This architecture allows Final Layer to support
many more validators than would be practical if every validator had to produce
full blocks, while still keeping all validators contributing meaningfully to
shard security and throughput.
